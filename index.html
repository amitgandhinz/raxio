Now that we've gotten together, let's collaborate.

Each morning before the talks start, everyone posts unconference session proposals on The Unconference Bulletin Board and vote for their favorites.

A multi-track afternoon unconference schedule will be posted during lunch, with session slots running in lock-step with conference talks to make it easy to move between conference talks and unconference sessions.

Half the fun of RAX.IO is meeting people in person and having informal discussions that energize and enhance one's point of view. At last year's RAX.IO retrospective, someone said that a cross-functional, cross-office networks discussion at dinner was more productive than all the problem solving of the past couple months.

There will be a late bus schedule for getting back to Austin, and a late night hack/game/hang room at the event hotel, and at least one festive evening keynote, too!

Make use of pair programming stations, join in at tutorials or ad-hoc whiteboard sessions, or find a corner to get in the zone.

All of the conference talks will be live streamed, with recordings uploaded to Racker TV afterwards.

Local Tech Crews will hold RAX.IO Re-Caps in each office to share take-aways and continue discussions locally.

Ticket FAQ

If I fill out the application am I registered and good to go?

Not quite. We have the go-ahead for 200 Rackers.

What if more than 200 Rackers apply? Many conferences use first-come-first-serve to determine attendance.

RAX.IO is not your normal conference. The application form, specifically the teams question, will be used to ensure that a diverse spectrum of people from across the company are able to come together.

What Business Units are eligible?

Last year was a huge success, so we're doubling in size and quadrupling business units. We're interested in representatives from every technology team within Product, Corp Dev, Private Cloud and Foundation/IT.

Business unit leaders of technical Rackers in SMB, ENTerprise and INTernational are selecting Rackers to fulfill limited guest slots. Please speak with your manager if you're interested.

Team representatives and nominations?

A primary goal of RAX.IO is diversity. This makes knowledge exchanges and relationship building more interesting and useful post-RAX.IO.

Therefore, in determining attendance, a large factor is seeking representatives from every product, project, speciality, team and subteam across the Rackspace technical community. This is not an exact science but a spirited striving.

Because we don't know all the teams, subteams, projects, etc in each BU, we'll need your help on the application to explain. Use the concepts that make the most sense to you. It's ok to be on multiple teams. It's ok to be on a subteam, or not.

After the application deadline, we may ask your team to nominate representatives. Sometimes the people closer to a situation have the best answer. Limited capacity is tough, but since RAX.IO is for Rackers, by Rackers, at least we're all working together on this one. Ultimately, we believe this apply-first-nominate-as-needed process will result in the best outcome, which includes not only meeting the immediate goals of RAX.IO, but also an increasingly inclusive, accessible and highly performant distributed technical community.

When will I know if I'm accepted?

RAX.IO speakers and attendees will be announced by early December.
